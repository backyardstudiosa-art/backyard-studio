When the Fog Begins to Lift

There are seasons in life when everything feels clouded. Thoughts blur together, direction feels uncertain, and the path ahead disappears into a quiet fog. In those moments, the instinct is often to search harder,plan more carefully, or try to force clarity through effort.

But slowly, gently, I am learning something different.

When the fog begins to lift and the skies ahead grow clear, it reminds me that I was never meant to see every step of the road. I do not have to understand every turn or solve every question before moving forward. Faith asks only that I lift my eyes.

“I lift up my eyes to the hills—
where does my help come from?
My help comes from the Lord,
the Maker of heaven and earth.”
Psalm 121:1–2

There is a deep comfort in those words. They remind me that help does not come from my own striving, nor from my ability to hold everything together. It comes from the One who made the hills themselves, the One who sees far beyond what my limited view can grasp.

Clear skies, I am discovering, rarely arrive through effort alone. They often come through surrender. Through the quiet moment of releasing control and trusting that God is already at work beyond what I can see.

And when that surrender happens, peace begins to settle in. The path may not be fully visible yet, but the heart becomes steady again.

Because He has always been my help. And He always will be.
