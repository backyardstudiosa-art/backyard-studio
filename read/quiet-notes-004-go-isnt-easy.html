Letting Go Isn’t Easy

When what you believed in begins to change.

— quiet readings

Letting go isn’t easy.
Not because we don’t know how to release,
but because what we’re letting go of once felt true.

Sometimes the hardest part is realizing
that what we believed in
was shaped by hope, not reality.
That we didn’t imagine everything —
we simply wanted to see the best,
and held onto the parts that felt safe, promising, alive.

It hurts when the picture you carried
slowly reveals its missing pieces.
When what you trusted
doesn’t reach back.
When what you thought was mutual
was only held on one side.

And still… listen.
When things feel off, they usually are.
Everything that chooses you
doesn’t always mean it’s meant for you.
The quiet knowing doesn’t arrive loudly —
it waits patiently for your attention.

Letting go, then, isn’t about forgetting.
It’s about loosening your grip on what never fully chose you.
It’s about accepting that love, meaning, or belonging
cannot be sustained by intention alone.

So if it feels heavy,
if your hands ache from holding on too long,
know this:
you’re not weak for struggling to release.
You were simply sincere in what you gave.

And with time, softly,
you’ll learn that letting go
isn’t the loss of what was imagined —
it’s the return to what is true.
